The following should be entered:
  • Provisions which are mentioned as being controlled, restricted discretionary, discretionary, or non-complying activites.
  • Performance standards that are mentioned as being breached (or not met). This may be explicit or the standard could be outlined as a minimum/maximum requirement with the activity's result also outlined (e.g. 'There is a minimum of two carparks required. The activity proposes to provide one carpark').

Some provisions may be at a finer level of detail than the drop-down options. For instance, if Rule 6.6.1.2 is breached but the drop-down box only includes Rule 6.6.1, then the available option (6.6.1) should be used.
The following should not be entered:
  • Provisions which are mentioned as being permitted activities.
  • Performance standards which are mentioned as being met.
  • Any matters of discretion.

If a provision is mentioned but not included in the drop-down list, then it does not need to be entered.
